If your team is searching for an aluminum die casting supplier in China, the biggest risk is not unit price. The real risk is selecting a partner that cannot hold quality and delivery once volume ramps up.

This checklist helps procurement and engineering teams shortlist suppliers that can support both pilot builds and long-term production.

![Die casting production line for sourcing evaluation](/img/automated-die-casting-production-line.webp "Evaluate process stability, not only piece price")

## 1. Confirm Process Capability Before Price Negotiation

Ask for objective data first:

- Machine tonnage range and shot size capability for your part family.
- Typical wall thickness window and achievable flatness.
- In-house CNC, CMM, and X-ray availability.
- Vacuum die casting readiness for sealing-critical housings.

If these basics are missing, a low quote usually becomes a high total cost after rework, delays, and warranty exposure.

## 2. Validate DFM Depth, Not Just a Sales Promise

A strong supplier should provide a DFM package that includes:

- Gate and overflow strategy.
- Draft, parting line, and ejector pin feasibility.
- Porosity risk zones and machining allowance.
- Recommended alloy path (for example ADC12 vs A380).

Use this benchmark guide: [How DFM analysis reduces die casting costs](https://alumcasting.com/how-dfm-analysis-reduces-die-casting-costs/).

## 3. Audit Quality System for Serial Production

For automotive, industrial, and e-mobility projects, require evidence of:

- APQP and PPAP execution flow.
- Incoming, in-process, and outgoing control plans.
- Leak test strategy when airtightness matters.
- Corrective action loop with measurable closure time.

Useful reference: [IATF 16949 high-tolerance automotive supplier](https://alumcasting.com/iatf-16949-high-tolerance-automotive-cnc-machining-supplier/).

## 4. Review Lead Time Risk From RFQ to SOP

Do not accept a single "tooling lead time" number. Ask for phase-by-phase timing:

- RFQ response and technical clarification.
- DFM freeze and tool design release.
- T1/T2 trial loops and dimensional closure.
- PPAP approval and SOP handoff.

This timeline approach prevents hidden schedule drift.

## 5. Compare Quotes on Total Landed Cost

Build your supplier comparison table around:

- Tooling CAPEX and expected maintenance plan.
- Piece price under realistic scrap assumptions.
- Secondary process cost (CNC, coating, leak test, packaging).
- Logistics model and Incoterms impact.

Cheapest piece price is often not the lowest landed cost.

## Quick RFQ Data Pack to Improve Quote Accuracy

Before sending RFQs, prepare:

- 2D drawing with critical characteristics.
- 3D model (STEP) and assembly context.
- Annual volume by phase (pilot, year 1, year 2).
- Surface finish and leak-tightness requirements.
- Target region and delivery terms.

With complete inputs, quality suppliers can usually return a usable quote and DFM direction much faster.
