For EV housings, choosing between magnesium and aluminum die casting is not only a material decision. It affects structural performance, corrosion strategy, tooling life, cycle time, and final program economics.

This guide gives sourcing and engineering teams a decision framework focused on production reality.

![EV die casting housing material decision](/img/ev-powertrain-die-cast-housing.webp "Material selection should align with performance and manufacturing risk")

## 1. Weight Reduction Potential

Magnesium is significantly lighter than aluminum and can deliver meaningful mass reduction in non-crash-critical housings and covers.

- Magnesium density is roughly 30% lower than common aluminum die casting alloys.
- In range-sensitive EV programs, this can improve system-level efficiency.

However, wall-thickness and rib strategy must be co-optimized through DFM, not copied directly from an existing aluminum design.

## 2. Corrosion and Surface Protection Strategy

Magnesium typically requires more deliberate corrosion protection:

- Conversion layers and sealing.
- MAO or equivalent surface engineering for harsh environments.
- Tight process control on pre-treatment and coating adhesion.

If your supply chain cannot stabilize this stack, aluminum may provide lower field risk.

## 3. Structural and Thermal Priorities

Material selection should follow your dominant requirement:

- If lightweight and damping are priority drivers, magnesium can be attractive.
- If thermal path stability and broad manufacturing familiarity are primary, aluminum often wins.

For thermal-critical battery components, review this reference: [EV battery housing die casting and thermal design](https://alumcasting.com/ev-battery-housing-die-casting/).

## 4. Tooling and Process Risk

Program teams often underestimate implementation risk:

- Supplier experience with magnesium melt handling and safety controls.
- Die temperature management and shot profile stability.
- Downstream machining and fixture strategy for thin walls.

A technically valid material still fails commercially when process capability is immature.

## 5. Total Cost View Instead of Piece Price Only

Evaluate full program cost:

- Tooling investment and expected die maintenance.
- Scrap and rework sensitivity.
- Surface-treatment and validation overhead.
- Warranty and field-risk cost.

The correct material is the one with the best total landed value under your quality target.

## Practical Selection Checklist

Before final freeze, confirm:

- Mass target and allowable envelope.
- Corrosion test standard and service environment.
- Airtightness requirement and leak-test method.
- Supplier capability for both casting and downstream finishing.
- Ramp plan from pilot to SOP.
