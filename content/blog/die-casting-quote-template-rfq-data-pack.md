Many die casting projects lose weeks in repeated quote revisions. The root cause is usually incomplete RFQ input, not supplier response speed.

This template gives procurement and engineering teams a practical RFQ structure that improves quote accuracy and shortens sourcing cycle time.

![Die casting RFQ and cost review workflow](/img/engineering-team-reviewing-cad-models.webp "Better RFQ input produces faster and more accurate quotes")

## What a Complete Die Casting RFQ Should Include

## 1. Technical Package

- 3D model in STEP format.
- 2D drawing with GD&T and critical dimensions.
- Leak-tightness requirement if applicable.
- Surface finish and cosmetic class expectations.
- Material preference (for example ADC12 or A380).

## 2. Volume and Program Plan

- Prototype quantity.
- Pilot build quantity.
- SOP monthly demand and annual run rate.
- Program life and EAU forecast.

Without volume context, suppliers cannot optimize tooling strategy or unit economics.

## 3. Quality and Validation Requirements

- Required capability targets for critical dimensions.
- Sampling plan and acceptance standard.
- Need for X-ray, CMM, CT, or leak test.
- PPAP level and documentation deliverables.

Reference process: [Die casting factory CMM and X-ray inspection](https://alumcasting.com/die-casting-factory-cmm-xray-inspection/).

## 4. Commercial and Logistics Terms

- Preferred Incoterm.
- Packaging standard and labeling rules.
- Payment terms and currency.
- Destination and freight mode assumptions.

This prevents quote gaps that appear only at PO stage.

## Standard Quote Output You Should Request

Ask each supplier to return the same structure:

- Tooling cost split by die set, inserts, and fixtures.
- Unit price at different annual volumes.
- Separate line items for CNC, surface treatment, and inspection.
- NRE assumptions and validity period.
- Lead-time chart from RFQ to SOP.

Uniform quote format makes supplier comparison much more reliable.

## Common RFQ Mistakes That Increase Total Cost

- Sending only a 3D model without critical tolerances.
- No clarity on leak-tightness or pressure-test requirement.
- Asking for price before agreeing DFM boundary conditions.
- Omitting expected annual volume and lifecycle demand.

To reduce these risks, pair the RFQ with an early DFM review: [Cost-down DFM design for die casting molds](https://alumcasting.com/cost-down-dfm-design-aluminum-die-casting-molds/).
